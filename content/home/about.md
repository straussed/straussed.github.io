I am a behavioral ecologist interested in the ecology and evolution of complex social systems. One particularly striking and widespread feature of social systems is that, across species, societies show persistent and structured inequality. I'm interested in understanding why inequality is so prevalent across species, what causes changes in inequality and social hierarchies, and how inequality influences the ecology and evolution of social organisms. For my PhD, I studied the [dynamics and stability of dominance hierarchies](https://besjournals.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1111/1365-2656.12951) in spotted hyenas across scales, from the [dynamic emergence of structured hierarchy among juveniles](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/812362v1) to the [intergenerational patterns in dominance](https://www.pnas.org/content/116/18/8919) that lead to [dynastic societies](https://www.pnas.org/content/116/18/8644). Since completing my PhD in 2019, I continue to remain an active member of the long-term [Mara Hyena Project](https://www.holekamplab.org/), but I'm also increasingly focused on using comparative methods to identify patterns in social structure across systems. My current research is aimed at implementing a comparative framework to understand the socioecology, structure, and dynamics of inequality across species. I am passionate about the importance of field work and organismal knowledge, the proliferation and advancement of quantitative tools in evolution and ecology, and the value of long-term research. I am currently a Postdoctoral Researcher affiliated with the [BEACON Center for the Study of Evolution in Action](https://www3.beacon-center.org/) at Michigan State University.
